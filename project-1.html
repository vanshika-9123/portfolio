Warehouse Group - Brand Identity

Project Overview

Warehouse Group is known for its lively and relaxed dining environment and has established itself as a standout in Canada’s bustling food industry. Despite its success, the brand faced an ongoing challenge — keeping up a visual and emotional connection with its diverse and growing audience. This issue has become more pronounced in an age where an effective digital presence is crucial for engaging customers and shaping how they see the brand.

My decision to focus on rebranding Warehouse Group came from two main observations. Firstly, because the brand has numerous locations across Canada, any updates to its branding would be impactful, making it an excellent opportunity for significant design improvements. Secondly, a preliminary look at the brand’s current state, particularly its website, showed multiple opportunities for improvement. The color schemes were inconsistent, and the website’s design, although functional, did not meet the modern and cohesive look expected by today’s tech-savvy consumers.

Creative Process

My creative process began with thorough research and planning, which provided a clear direction for the project. To kickstart the design phase, I delved into logo inspiration on platforms like Pinterest and Behance. Initially, my logo concepts leaned heavily towards traditional food industry aesthetics, but as the project progressed, they evolved to better align with the brand’s desired modern and approachable identity.

Next, I focused on defining the primary brand color, which I affectionately termed “spark yellow,” and complemented it with a secondary color — white — for versatility and simplicity. Given the prominence of the brand name, I opted for a clean and straightforward typography choice: Arial. This decision aimed to ensure readability and coherence across various applications while allowing the brand name to stand out prominently.
